🌟Introduction

When we are hosting an EC2 instance, AWS is able to decides which physical rack in their massive data center our server sits on. Most of the time, anywhere is fine. But what if you need two servers to talk to each other at high speed? Or what if you want to ensure that a single rack failure doesn’t take down your entire application? To solve these problems where placement groups come in. It allow you to influence the physical seating chart of your servers to optimize for either raw performance or extreme reliability.

The Three Strategies

Before we start to build, let’s read the three main ways you can organize your instances:

  1. Cluster: Tightly packed on the same rack for ultra-low latency. (The Ferrari: Fast but high risk if the rack fails).
  2. Spread: Each instance is on a totally different rack. (The Insurance Policy: Maximum safety for a few critical instances).
  3. Partition: Large groups of instances spread across logical partitions that don’t share hardware. (The Hadoop/Kafka Special: Resiliency at scale).

Step 1: Create the Placement Groups

Before launching instances, we need to define our seating rules.

  • Let’s navigate to the EC2 Dashboard.
  • In the left-hand sidebar, scroll down, you can see Network & Security, and click Placement Groups.
  • Click Create placement group, and let’s make three:
  • Name: my-high-performance-group | Strategy: Cluster.
  • Name: my-critical-group | Strategy: Spread (Set spread level to Rack).
  • Name: my-distributed-group | Strategy: Partition (Set Number of partitions to 4).
  • Now we created the Placement groups

Step 2: Launch an Instance into a Group

  • Now in the left sidebar, click on Instances and click Launch Instances.
  • Choose AMI (Amazon Linux 2) and instance type.
  • Scroll to the bottom and expand Advanced Details.
  • Find the Placement group name dropdown.
  • Select one of your new groups.
  • Launch the instance!

Step 3: Verification

When the instance starts running, click the instance and look at the Details tab. You will see the placement group field correctly showing the group you selected. This ensures that AWS has physically placed your server according to your specific strategy.

🧾 Conclusion

Placement Groups give you the architect’s touch over the physical layout of the cloud. Then, by selecting the default placement and choosing a strategy that fits your workload, you can significantly reduce network latency or protect your system from hardware disasters. It’s a simple setting with massive implications for your application’s stability and speed.
