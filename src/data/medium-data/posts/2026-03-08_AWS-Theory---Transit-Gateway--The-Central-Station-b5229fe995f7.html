🌟 Introduction

As your AWS footprint grows, managing network connections can start to feel like untangling a massive bowl of spaghetti. If you have ten VPCs and want them all to talk to each other, you’d need to set up and manage 45 individual peering connections! AWS Transit Gateway is the solution to this mesh madness. It acts as a central regional hub a star-shaped switchboard that connects thousands of VPCs and on-premises networks together. Instead of building a private highway between every single building, you connect every building to one central train station.

Network topologies can become complicated

1. The Hub-and-Spoke Model

The Transit Gateway uses a Hub-and-Spoke architecture. The Hub is the Transit Gateway itself, and the Spokes are your VPCs, VPNs, and Direct Connect links.

  • Transitive Routing: Unlike standard VPC Peering, Transit Gateway supports transitive routing. If VPC A is connected to the Hub and VPC B is also connected, they can talk to each other automatically through the Hub.
  • Centralized Control: You manage all your traffic rules in one place using Transit Gateway Route Tables. You can decide exactly which VPCs are allowed to see each other and which should remain isolated.

2. Supercharging VPN with ECMP

One of the coolest power user features of Transit Gateway is its ability to increase your VPN bandwidth using Equal-Cost Multi-Path (ECMP) routing.

  • The Problem: A standard AWS Site-to-Site VPN tunnel is capped at 1.25 Gbps. Even though a VPN connection gives you two tunnels, you usually only use one at a time for a single flow.
  • The Solution: When you connect your VPN to a Transit Gateway and enable ECMP, AWS can distribute your traffic across multiple tunnels simultaneously.
  • The Result: By adding more VPN connections (each with two tunnels), you can scale your bandwidth far beyond the 1.25 Gbps limit, effectively “bonding” them together to reach 10 Gbps or more.

3. Sharing Direct Connect Across Accounts

In large organizations, you might have one high-speed Direct Connect (DX) link but dozens of different AWS accounts. Transit Gateway makes it easy to share that expensive connection.

By linking your Direct Connect Gateway to a Transit Gateway, every VPC attached to that hub—regardless of which AWS account it belongs to—can use that single physical DX link to talk back to your corporate data center. You can manage this sharing easily using the AWS Resource Access Manager (RAM).

4. Key Facts for the Exam

If you’re prepping for an AWS certification, keep these Transit Gateway “keywords” in mind:

  • Multicast: Transit Gateway is the only service that supports IP Multicast in AWS.
  • Regional but Peered: It is a regional resource, but you can “peer” Transit Gateways across different regions to create a global network.
  • Cost: You pay a fee per “attachment” (per VPC/VPN/DX connected) plus a fee for every GB of data processed.

🧾 Conclusion

AWS Transit Gateway is the “grown-up” way to handle networking at scale. It transforms a complex web of peering into a clean, manageable star topology that grows with your business. Whether you need to connect 5,000 VPCs or just want to squeeze more speed out of your VPN, the Transit Gateway is the central brain of your cloud network.
