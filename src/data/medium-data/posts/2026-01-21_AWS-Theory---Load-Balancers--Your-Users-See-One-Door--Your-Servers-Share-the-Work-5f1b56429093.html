Introduction

To understand an Elastic Load Balancer (ELB), think of it as a smart hostess at a massive popular restaurant.

The Real-World Scenario

Imagine a restaurant that has five different kitchens in the back. All kitchens are capable of making the same food.

  • Without a Load Balancer: Customers, they don’t know which kitchen to choose. If they choose kitchen A, they might all crowd into kitchen A. The rest of the kitchens and the chefs are standing around doing nothing. If Kitchen A’s oven breaks, all those customers go hungry.
  • With a Load Balancer: There is one main front door. The Hostess (the Load Balancer) stands there. As customers walk in, she checks her clipboard and says, “Kitchen B is empty, go there!” or “Kitchen C is cleaning their stove, please go to Kitchen D.”

Why do you actually need it?

  1. A Single Point of Contact: Your users don’t need to know the IP addresses of your 50 different servers. They just remember one name (e.g., www.yourshop.com). The load balancer handles the hidden messy work of finding an available server in the background.
  2. The Sick Chef Protection (Health Checks): If one of your servers crashes, the load balancer detects it instantly. It stops sending traffic to that server until it’s healthy again. Your customers never see an error page. They just get sent to a working server.
  3. Invisible Maintenance: You can take a server offline to update its software in the middle of the day. The load balancer will just shift the “crowd” to the other servers while you work.
  4. It is integrated with many AWS offerings/services.
    • EC2, EC2 Auto Scaling Groups, Amazon ECS
    • AWS Certificate Manager (ACM), CloudWatch
    • Route 53, AWS WAF, AWS Global Accelerator

The 3 Types of AWS Load Balancers

Rule of thumb: 99 % of new projects → ALB (web) or NLB (everything else).

The Security Secret

Think of the Load Balancer as a Front Gate.

  • You allow the whole world to come to the Front Gate (the Load Balancer).
  • But your servers (the kitchens) are locked behind a fence. They will say, “Don’t talk to anyone unless they come from the Hostess.”

This is why we set the Security Group of the EC2 instances to only accept traffic from the Load Balancer. It makes your servers “invisible” to hackers on the public internet.

  • Load balancer security group → allow 80/443 from 0.0.0.0/0 (the internet)
  • EC2 instances security group → allow 80 from the load balancer’s security group (not from the internet!)

Conclusion

A load balancer is the Master Coordinator of your cloud. By using this, it ensures that no single server is stressed out, no user is left waiting, and no hardware failure ruins your day. It’s the single most important tool for turning a single server into a global, unbreakable system. You now know why every serious application on AWS sits behind a load balancer. It’s the difference between “works on my machine” and “works for 100k users while surviving failures.” It’s fully managed! 🚀
