🌟 Introduction

In the previous sections, we learned how to build a secure house (VPC) with locked rooms (private subnets). But eventually, the “owner” (you, the administrator) needs to get inside those locked rooms to perform maintenance or check on things. Since those rooms have no windows or doors facing the street, you need a secure way to “hop” from the outside world into the heart of your network. This is where the Bastion Host (also known as a Jump Server) comes into play.

1. What is a Bastion Host?

A Bastion Host is a special purpose EC2 instance that lives in your public subnet. Its only job is to act as a secure proxy or “bridge.” Instead of exposing all your sensitive application and database servers to the internet, you expose only this one, highly hardened server.

Think of it like a security kiosk at the entrance of a gated community. You can’t drive directly to a house. You must first stop at the kiosk, prove who you are to the guard, and only then are you allowed to proceed to the private residences.

2. The Connection Flow

To access a private instance, you perform a “double-hop”:

  1. First Hop: You SSH from your local computer into the Bastion Host using its public IP.
  2. Second Hop: Once inside the Bastion Host, you initiate a second SSH session to the Private Instance using its Private IP.
Security Tip: Never store your private SSH keys on the Bastion Host itself. If an attacker manages to compromise the Bastion, they would find your keys and gain access to everything. Instead, use a technique called SSH Agent Forwarding, which allows the Bastion to use the keys on your local computer without ever seeing or storing them.

3. Setting Up the Security Groups

For this to work, you have to configure your security groups (SGs) like a series of interlocking locks.

4. Why Not Just Give Everything a Public IP?

You might be tempted to just give every server a public IP to make your life easier. However, every public IP is a “door” that a hacker can try to kick down. By using a bastion host, you reduce your attack surface from dozens of servers down to just one. You can focus all your security efforts logging, monitoring, and frequent patching on that single gatekeeper.

🧾 Conclusion

The Bastion Host is a classic architectural pattern that balances high security with administrative necessity. It ensures that your most valuable assets remain invisible to the public internet while still being reachable by authorized staff. While modern alternatives like AWS Systems Manager Session Manager are becoming popular (because they don’t require an open port 22 at all!), understanding the bastion host is fundamental to mastering VPC security and “jump” logic.
