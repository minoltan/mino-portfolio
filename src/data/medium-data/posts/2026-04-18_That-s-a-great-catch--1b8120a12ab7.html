That’s a great catch! You’re absolutely right—Multi-Region keys are a fantastic modern solution for this exact scenario.

I used the 'Snapshot Copy' method in the example because it helps beginners understand two fundamental KMS rules:

1. Keys are regional: Most keys stay exactly where they are born.

2. Re-encryption: It shows how data 'changes locks' when moving between locations.

However, you're 100% correct. If you know from day one that you’ll be moving data between London and Sydney, a Multi-Region key is much more efficient. It keeps the same Key ID in both places, saves you the headache of managing different policies, and as you mentioned, you only pay for what you replicate.
