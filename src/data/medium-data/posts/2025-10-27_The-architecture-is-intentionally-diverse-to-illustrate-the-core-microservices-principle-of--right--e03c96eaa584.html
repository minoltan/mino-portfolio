The architecture is intentionally diverse to illustrate the core microservices principle of "right tool for the job." It is designed to show architectural flexibility (serverless, containers, traditional VM) and independent scaling/deployment, not just what's possible with AWS. Using different stacks for different services is a strategic choice to meet unique operational and development requirements.

You are absolutely right that a simple blog doesn't need three different AWS computing models (ECS, Lambda, EC2) and three different databases (DynamoDB, ElastiCache, RDS). However, the blog post's goal, as stated in the introduction and requirements, is not to build the simplest blog but to showcase the microservices pattern and its benefits.
