Introduction

Want to run massive workloads for pennies? EC2 Spot Instances let you use AWS’s spare capacity at discounts of up to 90% compared to On-Demand — but they can disappear with just 2 minutes’ warning. Perfect for batch processing, CI/CD, data analytics, or anything that can survive sudden restarts. In this no-fluff guide you’ll learn exactly how Spot pricing works, the four allocation strategies in Spot Fleets, how to never accidentally relaunch terminated instances, and why Spot is now safer and smarter than ever in 2025.

How Spot Pricing Actually Works

  • You set a maximum price you’re willing to pay (or just choose the default = On-Demand price).
  • AWS publishes a live Spot price per instance type + AZ that fluctuates like a stock market.
  • As long as the current Spot price ≤ your max → you keep the instance.
  • The moment it goes above → AWS gives you a 2-minute warning, then either stops or terminates the instance (you choose the behavior).

Real example (m5.large, us-east-1): On-Demand = $0.096/hour → Spot usually $0.020–$0.030 → 70–80% savings.

Spot Requests: One-Time vs Persistent (Exam Favorite!)

Critical rule: To permanently stop Spot instances:

  1. Cancel the Spot Request first
  2. Then terminate the instances (If you only terminate the instances, a persistent request will just spin up new ones!)

Pro Tips for 2025

  • Combine Spot + On-Demand in the same fleet (e.g., 80% Spot, 20% On-Demand as fallback)
  • Use the default max price (On-Demand) — you’ll almost never get interrupted
  • Enable Spot Instance interruption notices in EventBridge or CloudWatch Events to gracefully drain tasks
  • Spot + EKS/EKS Fargate or ECS is now seamless with built-in replacement

Conclusion

Spot Instances used to be “scary cheap.” Today they’re smart cheap — with persistent requests, diversified fleets, and price-capacity-optimized strategies, most companies run huge chunks of their compute on Spot without anyone noticing the interruptions. Start small: replace one batch job with Spot tomorrow and watch your bill drop 70–90%. The savings are real, the tools are mature, and your future self (and CFO) will thank you.
