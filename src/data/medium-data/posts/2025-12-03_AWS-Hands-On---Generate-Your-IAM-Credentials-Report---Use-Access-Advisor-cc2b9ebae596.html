Introduction

You’ve heard the theory — now let’s do the actual security audit that every serious AWS account needs. In under five minutes you can generate the IAM Credentials Report (the full credential health check of your entire account) and open the IAM Access Advisor to see exactly which services your users and roles are actually using. These two free tools are your fastest path to finding missing MFA, ancient access keys, and permission bloat. Grab a coffee, open the IAM console, and let’s run both right now.

Step 1: Generate & Download the IAM Credentials Report (60 seconds)

  1. IAM → Dashboard (left sidebar)
  2. Scroll down to “Generate credential report” → click it
  3. Wait ~10 seconds → click “Download credential report”
  4. Open the CSV — you’ll instantly see columns like:

Real example from a fresh training account:

Root = MFA active ✓
Access keys = none ✓ 
Mino = MFA inactive ✗ 
Access keys exist and never rotated ✗ →

Immediate action items created in 10 seconds.

Step 2: Open IAM Access Advisor & Kill Permission Creep (2 minutes)

  1. IAM → Users → click your username (or any user/role)
  2. Tab →Last Accessed
  3. Boom — you now see every single AWS service this identity can touch and the last time it actually did.

Sample discoveries:

  • Your developer still has AdministratorAccess but hasn’t touched S3 in 412 days
  • A role has rds:* permissions even though the app was migrated to DynamoDB 18 months ago
  • 37 pages of services like “Alexa for Business” and “AWS IoT SiteWise” that were granted “just in case” and never used

Click any service (e.g., EC2) → it even tells you exactly which policy granted the permission (AdministratorAccess, custom policy, etc.). One click → remove or replace with a tighter policy.

Immediate Wins You Can Claim Today

Conclusion

Five minutes with the IAM Credentials Report and Access Advisor just gave you a complete security posture snapshot that most companies pay expensive consultants thousands for. You now know exactly who has MFA, who is hoarding ancient keys, and who has permissions to services they’ve never touched. Do this exercise today, fix the red flags, then schedule it quarterly (or automate it with a Lambda). Your future self — and any auditor or security team — will thank you. Go run those reports right now. Your account will be dramatically safer before your coffee gets cold.
