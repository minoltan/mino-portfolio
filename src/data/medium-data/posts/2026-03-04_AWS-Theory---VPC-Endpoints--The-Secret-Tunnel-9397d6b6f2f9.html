🌟 Introduction

Imagine you have a private office (your VPC) and you need to send files to a massive warehouse (S3 or DynamoDB). Normally, your delivery person has to leave the building, drive through heavy city traffic (the public internet), pay some tolls (NAT Gateway costs), and then enter the warehouse through the front door. VPC Endpoints change that by building a private, secure elevator that goes directly from your office to the warehouse. Your data never leaves the “building” (the AWS network), it’s faster, and in many cases, it saves you a significant amount of money on tolls.

1. Why Use a VPC Endpoint?

By default, services like S3, DynamoDB, and SNS are “public” services. This means even if your EC2 instance is in a private subnet, it has to find a path out to the internet (usually via a NAT Gateway) to talk to them.

The Problems with the “Public” Route:

  • Cost: NAT Gateways charge per GB of data processed. Transferring large S3 backups this way gets expensive fast.
  • Security: Traffic traverses the public internet, which might be a compliance concern for sensitive data.
  • Performance: Going through a NAT Gateway adds extra “hops” to the journey.

2. The Two Types: Interface vs. Gateway

AWS offers two distinct flavors of endpoints. Choosing the right one is a common “trick” on AWS exams.

Interface Endpoints (Powered by PrivateLink)

These provision an Elastic Network Interface (ENI)—essentially a virtual network card with a private IP directly in your subnet.

  • How it works: You talk to the private IP of the ENI, and it whisks your data to the service.
  • Security: You use Security Groups to control who can talk to the ENI.
  • Services: Supports almost everything (SNS, CloudWatch, Kinesis, etc.).
  • Cost: There is an hourly charge and a data processing fee.

Gateway Endpoints

These are a bit more “old school” but incredibly efficient. They don’t use private IPs or ENIs.

  • How it works: You update your Route Table to point traffic destined for S3 or DynamoDB to the “Gateway.”
  • Security: Managed via Endpoint Policies (not security groups).
  • Services: ONLY supports Amazon S3 and DynamoDB.
  • Cost: FREE! This is why it’s usually the preferred choice for S3/DynamoDB.

3. Interface vs. Gateway for S3?

Since S3 supports both, which should you use?

  • Choose Gateway: 99% of the time. It’s free and simpler to manage.
  • Choose Interface: Only if you need to access S3 from outside the VPC (like an on-premises data center via Direct Connect) or from a different AWS region.

🧾 Conclusion

VPC Endpoints are the cornerstone of a secure, “internet-free” architecture. By keeping your traffic within the AWS backbone, you reduce your attack surface and often slash your monthly networking bill. Just remember: if it’s S3 or DynamoDB, look for the Gateway; for everything else, plug in an Interface.
